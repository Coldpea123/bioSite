Inspiration

Tuan Trinh is an extremely hardworking and motivated individual whose persistence led him to where he is today. His career is not necessarily an easy one to get to or achieve. The pharmaceutical career path requires a Bachelor’s degree at an accredited institution in a field such as Biology or Chemistry, taking the Pharmacy College Admission Test (PCAT), applying to a pharmacy school that is accredited by the Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education to earn a doctoral degree (PHARM D), completing a formal internship or residency program as well as experiential learning in pharmacy practice settings, needing to take two licensure exams and satisfying any prerequisites required by the state in which you will practice in, and even after becoming a full-fledged pharmacist, they are required to participate in continuing pharmacy education or re-licensing practices to continue professional development opportunities.

Getting admitted into pharmacy schools requires specific prerequisite coursework which include: Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Math/Statistics, English/Communications, and Economics. Once admitted into pharmacy school, courses that will be taken to further your pharmaceutical education include: biochemistry, pharmacology, healthcare management, chemotherapy, pharmaceutical measurements, biostatistics, and pathology. Then before you can begin your pharmacy career, completing internships and residency programs are necessary, with the hours needed depending on state licensing requirements. Then followed by two pharmacist licensure exams and satisfy pharmacist licensure requirements set by the state. There are also other roles in which you could take on in the realm of pharmacy, these include: the pharmacist, pharmacy technician, staff pharmacist, director of pharmacy, clinical pharmacist, pharmacologist, etc.

Biography

Tuan Trinh, born on June 29th, 1991 in Phan Rang, Vietnam, did not have an easy life. Tuan’s family was poor and struggled to survive day by day. Living primarily with his father in a small hut for the majority of his early adolescence, he got by through balancing school-life/studies and also cooking or the caretaking of his younger half-siblings. Here, he learned how to cook meals for his family based on anything they could find or grow in their small garden.

It wasn't until the age of 11, that his mother, who had moved over to the United States, was able to extend an invitation over to him to move to Long Beach, California with her as to provide a better life for the both of them. New struggles that he partook in was learning an entirely new language and culture away from everything that he had known previously. He spent years building his English skills and getting through American education, while still helping his family in America with housework/chores and cooking while his mother and relatives were away at work every day. After a few years of living there and finishing middle school and a bit of high school, he and his family moved to Victorville, CA where most of his family would continue to live to this day.

At Silverado High School, he gained an appreciation for the sport of tennis where he competed with his High School team. Although wanting to focus on his studies, he was only able to make occasional time for tennis as a hobby throughout his higher education. He attended Victor Valley Community College for X years until he realized that he wanted to pursue a career in pharmacy. From there, he transferred to Long Beach State University where he achieved his bachelor’s degree in Chemistry. For pharmacy school, Tuan had gotten admitted to an accelerated program out in Massachusetts, where he would spend 3 years away from friends and family to achieve his Doctor of Pharmacy degree.

Now, Tuan works as a pharmacist manager in Victorville, where he spends a lot of time with his family and dog. However, his aspirations continue to grow as he still intends to pursue higher responsibilities and continue to work hard, aiming to live a most fulfilling life full of countless experiences.
